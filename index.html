Success stories


Career disillusionment

A Dutch client was a talented florist yet very unhappy working as a bar manager . To complicate things she had become romantically involved with her boss who was prone to undermine her self confidence on professional basis but also rocked her self esteem. Childcare was a problem due to the unsocial hours. I supported her in finding her true passion in life and discover her vocational strengths. This is turn led to securing a role as flower importer from Holland to numerous supermarkets. A home based occupation brought her closer to her children and gave her the time and confidence to find a new partner.

Debt Management and return to work

My client and partner had been hit hard by the recession and both made redundant. With a family to raise and bills to pay, finding suitable work was causing difficulties in their relationship. Whilst my client had secured a part-time job her husband was reluctantly sending off job applications that she selected for him, based on a strict criteria of salary, proximity and hours. Her true passion was to pursue training to become a web designer so she could work from home and be present for her children. Whist he wanted to return to the licensing trade.Together we devised a payment plan for the course and an incentive for the husband to find suitable employment. Within weeks the £5 a month offer for the course was accepted. She gave her blessing for her husband to manage a bar and on the nights he worked she completed her college work. Both objectives were achieved and harmony was restored.

Eating disorder

A highly intelligent and stunningly beautiful young woman came to me for guidance in finding a life partner. We explored and addressed the underlying issues which transpired to be an eating disorder trigged by an emotional event. Over time we worked on self confidence and met and achieved important milestones until she had the confidence to make the necessary changes that led to her attracting a different kind of partner who met all her needs.

The SIO


The role of SIO: A Senior Investigating Officer is an experienced detective who leads investigations into major and serious crime. Such as murder, manslaughter, kidnap and stranger rapes. There is a national accreditated programme ensures that all elements of an investigation are used to good effect before ebing signed off and included on a national database/ accredited programme of training to ensure that an SIO has used a wide range of investigative techniques including sensitive and covert tactics. Whilst working toward accreditation an SIO works closely with a mentor until fully trained and experienced to effectively direct investigations.

Appointing trained staff for key roles such as exhibits, family liaison, disclosure, analyst, intelligence, forensic manager, suspect and witness interviewing, rape victim liaison etc. Setting investigation strategies such as CCTV strategy and interview strategy directing the focus of the investigation so for example house to house enquiries and making the decision to give a person suspect status.
